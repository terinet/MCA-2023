Piano roll

X-Axis: Represents time, with the leftmost part of the roll indicating the beginning and the rightmost part indicating the end of the musical passage.
Y-Axis: Represents pitch, with higher positions indicating higher pitches and lower positions indicating lower pitches.

Pitch histogram

A pitch histogram is a graphical representation that illustrates the distribution of pitch or musical notes within a piece of music.
In the context of music analysis, pitch refers to the perceived frequency of a sound, determining its musical note.

X-Axis: Represents the different pitches or musical notes.
Y-Axis: Represents the frequency or occurrence of each pitch.

According to this histogram, the most common pitch in "Underwater" is D3

Scatter plot of pitches

This plot explores the relationship between the duration of musical notes and their corresponding pitches.
This type of plot can provide insights into the rhythmic and pitch patterns within a musical composition.

X-Axis: Represents the duration of each musical note, measured in quarter lengths and presented on a logarithmic scale.
Y-Axis: Represents the musical pitch of each note. Higher positions on the y-axis correspond to higher pitches, and lower positions correspond to lower pitches.
